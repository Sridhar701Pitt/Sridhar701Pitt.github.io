# The Craft of Research (Booth et al.) – Prose and Chapter 1

This article and coming ones incorporate my understanding of ‘The Craft
of Research’ book by Booth et al.

## Why am I reading this book?

My one important goal in my life is to do research and writing research
papers or articles is very important and useful. My English professor
recommended me to read this book for my research writing class and
though it would be a good idea to blog about it as I go through reading
this book. Lets get started.

## Vocab for today

1.  *Thesis* – A statement or theory that is put forward as a premise to
    be maintained or proved.
    
    1.  Most current guides acknowledge that researchers rarely move in
        a straight line from finding a topic to stating a *thesis* to
        filling in note cards to drafting and revision

2.  *Reservations* – A statement that limits or restricts some claim
    
    1.  How to anticipate the *reservations* of thoughtful but critical
        readers and then respond appropriately.

3.  *Dupes* – Victims of deception
    
    1.  Without trustworthy published research, we all would be locked
        in the opinions of the moment, prisoners of what we alone
        experience or *dupes* to whatever we’re told.

4.  *Charlatans* – A person falsely claiming to have special knowledge
    or skill; a fraud
    
    1.  But at some point, you will join a conversation that, at its
        best, can help you and your community free us from ignorance,
        prejudice, and the half-baked ideas that so many *charlatans*
        try to impose on us.

5.  *First-rate* – of the best class or quality; excellent
    
    1.  Even if you turn out a *first-rate* paper, it may be read not by
        an eager world but only by your teacher.

6.  *Short-change* – cheat someone by not returning him enough money.
    
    1.  This book will help you do that, but you’ll short-change
        yourself if that’s all you do.

## Notes

1.  Research is hard work, but like any challenging job done well, both
    its process and its results can bring great satisfaction. No small
    part of that satisfaction comes form knowing that your work sustains
    the fabric of a community of people who share your interests,
    especially when you discover something that you believe can improve
    your readers’ lives by changing what and how they think.

2.  You can’t know how good your ideas are until you separate them from
    the swift and muddy flow of thought and fix them in an organized
    form that you – and our readers – can study.

3.  In short, we write to remember more accurately, understand better,
    and evaluate what we think more objectively. (And as you will
    discover, the more you write, the better you read.)

4.  But it would be a feeble education that did not change you at all,
    and the deeper your education, the more it will change the “you”
    that you are or want to be. That’s why it is so important to choose
    carefully what you study and with whom.

## My thinking

1.  I’ve very less experience reading books (usually love to read
    comics) with lots of text. In most situations, what I read is
    usually not comprehended by my mind. One trick that worked is to
    imagine myself in the situation and objects mentioned in the text
    and read along just like watching a movie (here you are imagining
    the movie in your mind while reading the text). This quickly spurs
    interest in me to read further and I comprehend a lot better (maybe
    that’s because I’m little bit of a visual learner). This trick also
    helps if you are distracted while you read.
